## Translation Analysis

**translation:**

- He was despised and rejected by men; a man of sorrows, and acquainted with grief. And as one from whom people hide their faces, he was despised—so we did not esteem him.

**notes:**

- Key nuance: וַחֲדַ֣ל אִישִׁ֔ים (“and [one who] ceases/has his place removed among men”) is commonly understood in Isaiah 53 as “rejected/left out of society.” The phrase כְּמַסְתֵּר פָּנִים מִמֶּנּוּ (“as one from whom faces are hidden from him”) conveys social disgrace—people avert their gaze. The closing line וְלֹ֥א חֲשַׁבְנֻֽהוּ means “we did not think/consider/esteem him,” i.e., the community failed to recognize his worth.
