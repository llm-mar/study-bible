## Author Analysis

**author:**

- Traditionally associated with the Book of Isaiah; more specifically, scholars often place Isaiah 53 in the later layer of Isaiah (often called Deutero-Isaiah), suggesting an anonymous prophet/scribe from the Babylonian exile period rather than the historical Isaiah son of Amoz.

**thoughts:**

The motivation appears devotional/interpretive—portraying suffering as purposeful (e.g., for redemption or divine plan) and encouraging the audience to trust in God despite persecution.

**audience:**
