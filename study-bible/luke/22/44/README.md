**alternate_translations:**

| And he was in agony and prayed more intensely; and his sweat appeared like drops of blood, trickling down to the ground. | “προσηύχετο ἐκτενέστερον” is best rendered as “prayed more earnestly/intensely.” “καταβαίνοντες ἐπὶ τὴν γῆν” can be rendered as “trickling/falling down to the ground,” preserving the image of downward flow. |

**notes:**

- This passage describes intense suffering and the phenomenon sometimes called “hematidrosis” (sweat resembling blood). The phrase “ὡσεὶ θρόμβοι αἵματος” is vivid: literally “like clots of blood,” though some English versions choose “drops of blood” to convey the outward appearance. The overall meaning is that his distress was severe and his sweat took on a bloodlike appearance, descending to the ground.
